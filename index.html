My favorite places

Las Ramblas

The heartbeat of the city is a pedestrian-only street and one of the most popular places to visit when in Barcelona. Filled with tourists the Ramblas is one of the most common places to get pick-pocketed but as with any big city, you need to be careful and follow basic travel safety policy of being aware.

Best way to travel in Barcelona

  • Barcelona International Airport is the nearest airport to the city and is 13 km away. It takes 30 minutes by car to reach the city from the airport.

  • Taxi from Barcelona airport There is no flat fee for this taxi ride and a trip to the city center can range between 35€ and 40€. Barcelona’s taxis are easily identified by their yellow and black exterior and a safe and quick way to travel in the city.

  • Metro A modern and well-connected underground system with eight metro lines that can get you pretty much everywhere in the city.

  • UBER A good way to get to the city from the airport and one of the cheapest fares that you can avail in Europe.

  • Walking + Biking Once within the city Barcelona is very bike and walking friendly city so make the most of these.

What to eat in Barcelona

Tapas

Barcelona is famous for some of the Best Tapas. Spanish cuisines is famous for their Tapas – which is basically mini portions of single dishes that are meant to be snacked on in bars/restaurants. You can easily make a meal out of sampling multiple kinds of tapas.

Glossary of Common Tapas Dishes

  • Allioli - Garlic mayo, no matter how ‘traditionally Catalan’ the waiter tells you it is
  • Patatas bravas - Patatas bravas, or fried potatoes topped with a spicy bravas sauce and all i oli, a garlic mayonnaise.
  • Pan con tomate - bread with tomato, is undoubtedly the most simple and staple item of Catalan cuisine, and an absolute must-try tapa in Barcelona
  • Anchovies - Anchoas, or anchovies, make for a popular tapa in Barcelona among locals.

Paella

A trip to Spain would be incomplete without tasting the famous paella. The iconic rice and seafood dish originating from Valencia and was originally made with beans and meat instead of fish a shellfish. For the best Spanish food in Barcelona – visit the historic fishing village of Barceloneta. Barceloneta is nestled between the open sea on the one side and the Marina Port Vell on the other. Barceloneta is famous for its seafood restaurants and classic tapas bars, many of which have stood here for decades.
